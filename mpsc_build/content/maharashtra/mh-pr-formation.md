## Overview
The State of Maharashtra was formed on **1 May 1960** (celebrated as *Maharashtra Din*) by carving a separate Marathi-speaking state out of the bilingual Bombay State. It was the culmination of the **Samyukta Maharashtra Movement**, a mass agitation demanding a unified state for all Marathi-speakers with Mumbai (Bombay) as its capital. This is a foundational, high-frequency MPSC topic — it links the linguistic reorganisation of Indian states with Maharashtra's modern political identity.

## In-Depth
### Background — Linguistic Reorganisation
After Independence, demands grew for states based on language. The **Dhar Commission (1948)** and **JVP Committee (1949, Jawaharlal Nehru–Vallabhbhai Patel–Pattabhi Sitaramayya)** initially opposed purely linguistic states. The death of **Potti Sriramulu (1952)** after a fast forced the creation of Andhra (1953) and the appointment of the **States Reorganisation Commission (SRC, 1953)** under Fazl Ali (members: H.N. Kunzru, K.M. Panikkar).

### The Bombay Problem
The SRC (report 1955) recommended a bilingual Bombay State rather than splitting it, mainly because of cosmopolitan **Bombay city** — claimed by both Marathi and Gujarati speakers. This triggered intense agitation.

### Samyukta Maharashtra Movement
- The **Samyukta Maharashtra Samiti** was formed in 1956 (key figures: **Keshavrao Jedhe, S.M. Joshi, Acharya Atre, S.A. Dange, Prabodhankar Thackeray**).
- Acharya Atre's newspaper *Maratha* and powerful oratory mobilised public opinion.
- Police firing on agitators in Mumbai (1955–56) killed **105 protesters**, commemorated at **Hutatma Chowk (Martyrs' Square), Flora Fountain, Mumbai**.
- A parallel **Mahagujarat Movement** demanded a separate Gujarat.

### Creation
The **Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960** split Bombay State into **Maharashtra** (with Mumbai as capital) and **Gujarat**, effective **1 May 1960**. Yashwantrao Chavan became the **first Chief Minister** of Maharashtra.

## Maharashtra Connection
This is a core Maharashtra identity topic. It explains why **1 May** is a state holiday, the symbolism of **Hutatma Chowk**, and the political prominence of leaders like Y.B. Chavan ("Shilpkar of modern Maharashtra"). It also underlies later issues — the **Maharashtra–Karnataka border dispute (Belgaum/Belagavi)** stems from this reorganisation.

## Key Facts
- Formation date: **1 May 1960** (Maharashtra Din).
- Created by: **Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960**.
- Bombay State split into **Maharashtra + Gujarat**.
- **105 martyrs** — Hutatma Chowk, Mumbai.
- First CM: **Yashwantrao Chavan**.
- SRC chair: **Fazl Ali** (1953, report 1955).
- Samyukta Maharashtra Samiti formed: **1956**.

## Memory Aids
- "**1960 → Bombay breaks into Maha + Guj**." Both states born the same day.
- **Hutatma = 105** (martyrs). "Atre's pen, Chavan's chair."
- SRC = **F-A-K-P** (Fazl Ali, Kunzru, Panikkar).

## Exam Traps
- Do **not** confuse the **Mahagujarat Movement** (for Gujarat) with the **Samyukta Maharashtra Movement** (for Maharashtra).
- The SRC (1955) **opposed** splitting Bombay — the split came later via the 1960 Act, not the SRC itself.
- Yashwantrao Chavan was the first CM of *Maharashtra* (1960); he had earlier been CM of *bilingual Bombay State*.
